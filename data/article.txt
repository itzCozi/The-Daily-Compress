As the world changes, so do the mental health issues that children and teens face. While some mental health issues 
like anxiety and depression have always been around, others are on the rise. Here are some of the most common mental health 
issues that children and teens are facing today. Anxiety is one of the most common mental health issues that 
children and teens face. According to the Anxiety and Depression Association of America, anxiety disorders are the 
most common mental illness in the United States, affecting 40 million adults. And children are not immune to anxiety disorders. 
In fact, one in eight children suffer from an anxiety disorder. There are many different types of anxiety 
disorders, each with its own set of symptoms. But in general, people with anxiety disorders feel excessive levels 
of fear, worry, and stress. This can interfere with their daily lives, making it difficult to go to school, work, or even 
leave the house. Depression is another common mental health issue that children and teens face. According to the 
National Institute of Mental Health, depression is a common mental disorder that causes people to feel sad, lost, hopeless, and worthless. 
It is more than just a bout of the blues. Depression is a serious mental illness that can interfere with a persons ability to 
function in their daily lives. While depression can occur at any age, it is most common in adolescents and young adults. 
According to the National Institute of Mental Health, about 3 million teens aged 12 to 17 suffer from depression each year. 
Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a mental health disorder 
that is characterized by problems with focus, hyperactivity, and impulsivity.
